# AEO Optimization Criteria

Evaluate every article draft against these criteria before delivering. Score each **PASS** or **FAIL** with a brief reason.

## Critical

| Criterion | Check |
|-----------|-------|
| **Direct answer in first 150 words** | Does the opening answer the target prompt directly and completely — no preamble, no "In this article…"? |
| **Quotable definition** | Is there at least one clear, self-contained 1–2 sentence definition that AI can extract verbatim? |

## High

| Criterion | Check |
|-----------|-------|
| **Question-phrased headings** | Are H2/H3 headings phrased as questions that match real user prompts? |
| **Extractable structured data** | Does the article contain comparison tables, numbered steps, or structured lists that AI engines can pull directly? |
| **Unique / proprietary data** | Does the article include data, case studies, measurements, or angles that exist nowhere else on the web? |
| **FAQ section** | Is there a FAQ section with 2+ Q&A pairs targeting high-probability AI prompts? |

## Medium

| Criterion | Check |
|-----------|-------|
| **E-E-A-T signals** | Are there first-person experience markers ("we tested…"), author credentials, cited sources, and publish/update dates? |
| **Real-world scenarios with specifics** | Does the article include concrete user scenarios with exact numbers, steps, or outcomes — not vague generalities? |
| **Word count 800–1500** | Is the article between 800 and 1500 words? Shorter is fine if the topic warrants it, but flag if outside range. |
| **Short paragraphs** | Are paragraphs kept to 3–4 sentences max? Long walls of text reduce extractability. |

## Passing threshold

- All **critical** and **high** criteria must pass before delivering.
- **Medium** failures are acceptable when the brand's voice demands it (e.g. the brand never uses FAQ sections).
- If any critical or high criterion fails → revise the draft and re-evaluate.
